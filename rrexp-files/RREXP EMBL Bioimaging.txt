# European Molecular Biology Laboratory (EMBL)

## Euro-BioImaging EMBL-Node

---

## Description

The Euro-BioImaging EMBL-Node, hosted at EMBL Heidelberg, is a key European hub for advanced light and electron microscopy. It offers scientists access to cutting-edge imaging technologies and expertise, fostering innovation through its strong collaboration with industry partners. The Node plays a central role in facilitating dialogue between developers and users of imaging technologies, ensuring optimal alignment of research needs and instrument capabilities.

The Node supports a wide range of applications in life sciences by offering both access to high-end equipment and in-depth technical guidance. Researchers are supported throughout the experimental workflow‚Äîfrom project planning and sample preparation to image acquisition and analysis.

### Services Provided

- Project planning for customized experimental design
- Sample preparation suited to a wide array of imaging modalities
- Microscope selection guidance and hands-on instrument use
- Image processing, analysis, and visualization support
- Basic and advanced training in microscopy techniques (e.g., FRAP, FRET, CLEM, STED)

These services are provided with the goal of empowering researchers in the use of high-resolution, dynamic, and correlative imaging to study biological processes at multiple scales.

## Instrument Highlights

### High-throughput Microscopy

The Node supports automated workflows tailored for genome-scale siRNA screening and high-content assays. These workflows integrate automated sample preparation, high-speed image acquisition, and real-time data analysis. Capabilities include:

- Multi-color 3D time-lapse microscopy
- Quantitative FRAP and FCCS
- Intelligent image mining across large datasets

## Specialties and Expertise of the Node

The EMBL Node excels in integrating high-throughput screening with quantitative imaging. Its strength lies in end-to-end workflows that include:

- Automated image acquisition and screening
- Quantitative analysis for large-scale studies (e.g., siRNA screens)
- Real-time data mining and interpretation

In addition, the Node is actively engaged in pre-commercial instrument development and software tool creation in partnership with industrial collaborators. It is a reference site for evaluation and beta testing of new imaging technologies and accessories.

---

## Additional Services

Researchers at the EMBL Node benefit from a comprehensive range of support services, including:

- Custom protocol development and methodological setup
- Instrument maintenance and expert technical assistance
- Extensive training in microscopy hardware and software
- Probe and animal preparation facilities
- Dedicated animal facilities, wet lab infrastructure, and storage rooms
- Image data management and remote analysis
- Workstations and seminar rooms for training
- Training in optical clearing techniques for enhanced tissue transparency

Note: Availability of services may vary depending on site capacity.
